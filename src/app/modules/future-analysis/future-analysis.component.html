Future State Analysis

Criminal and Anti-Social Use of E-Mobility in the UK

2024 Analysis NPCC Report Predictive Analysis

1. Introduction

The National Police Chiefs' Council (NPCC) coordinates policing strategies across the UK to ensure effective crime prevention and public safety. NPCC has expressed concerns on the increasing crime rate of e-mobility (electric bike, electric scooter, and electric motorcycle) in the UK. The initial current state analysis conducted revealed the systemic challenges of NPCC, which include:

Increasing surge of e-mobility crimes across UK.

Unavailability of a centralized Database

Unstandardized and inconsistent reporting of e-mobility crimes across the whole police force.

Illegal purchase of e-mobiles from the grey market

Additionally, the analysis of e-mobility crime between 2021 and 2023 across major cities revealed the trends of the offences, the crime hotspots, and the most used e-mobility devices. This future state design aims to present a strategic, data-driven approach that aligns with national policing goals, leverages digital and location-based tools to reduce crime, and ultimately enhances public safety. In response to the current challenges, the proposed future state outlines a comprehensive plan that includes increasing public awareness on the lawful use of e-mobility devices, expanding police patrols during high-risk periods, and delivering consistent training for officers to standardize reporting procedures. It also advocates for implementing an integrated, real-time crime reporting platform and introducing clear legal frameworks. Together, these measures are intended to strengthen collaboration between the public and police, improve the quality of reported data, and ensure greater accountability from users, retailers, and manufacturers within the e-mobility sector.
